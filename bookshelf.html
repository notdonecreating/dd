Bookshelf Favourites

Literature that has shaped my thinking and perspective

I don't tend to read much fiction, though I'd like to. Almost all fiction listed here are childhood reads. I don't necessarily like every book for the main message it purports to give. But every book here left me with something lasting. I haven't reread most of the fiction as an adult so I can't recommend them with certainty, but the others I recommend wholeheartedly.
Philosophy & Meaning
Man's Search for Meaning
Viktor E. Frankl
Man's Search for Meaning
By Viktor E. Frankl
A profound exploration of finding purpose even in the most difficult circumstances. Frankl's experiences in Nazi concentration camps formed the basis for his approach to discovering meaning in all forms of existence, even the most brutal ones.
The Courage to Be
Paul Tillich
The Courage to Be
By Paul Tillich
An examination of anxiety and courage in the modern world. Tillich's analysis of how we face the anxiety of fate and death, emptiness and meaninglessness, and guilt and condemnation has deeply influenced my understanding of the human condition.
The Confessions of St. Augustine
Saint Augustine
The Confessions of St. Augustine
By Saint Augustine
An introspective masterpiece exploring the journey from worldly desires to spiritual awakening. Augustine's candid reflections on his own struggles and transformation continue to resonate across centuries.
Mere Christianity
C.S. Lewis
Mere Christianity
By C.S. Lewis
A thoughtful defense of core Christian beliefs that transcends denominational boundaries. Lewis's logical approach and accessible metaphors make complex theological concepts remarkably clear.
The Hero with a Thousand Faces
Joseph Campbell
The Hero with a Thousand Faces
By Joseph Campbell
A comparative exploration of mythology that reveals the common patterns in hero stories across cultures. Campbell's concept of the monomyth has influenced my understanding of narrative, meaning, and the human journey.
Jung's Red Book
Carl Jung
Jung's Red Book
By Carl Jung
A deeply personal record of Jung's confrontation with his unconscious. This visually stunning and psychologically profound work represents Jung's attempt to understand the universal patterns underlying the human psyche.
Technology & Society
Technopoly
Neil Postman
Technopoly
By Neil Postman
A prescient critique of technology's domination of culture and thought. Though written before the internet age, Postman's warnings about surrendering culture to technology feel more relevant than ever.
The Glass Cage
Nicholas Carr
The Glass Cage
By Nicholas Carr
An exploration of automation's human consequences. Carr's nuanced look at how automation changes our relationship with our work, our environment, and ourselves has influenced my thinking on how we should design technology.
The World-Ending Fire
Wendell Berry
The World-Ending Fire
By Wendell Berry
A collection of essays that articulates a profound vision of sustainable living and local economics. Berry's insights on community, agriculture, and our relationship with the land offer an alternative to technological solutionism.
Zero to One
Peter Thiel
Zero to One
By Peter Thiel
A contrarian perspective on innovation and startup building. Thiel's emphasis on creating new categories rather than competing in existing ones has shaped my thinking about genuine progress.
The Prosperity Paradox
Clayton M. Christensen
The Prosperity Paradox
By Clayton M. Christensen, Efosa Ojomo, and Karen Dillon
A fresh approach to addressing poverty through market-creating innovations. The authors' focus on developing solutions from within rather than imposing external aid has influenced my thinking on sustainable development.
Wonder & Creativity
The Zen of Seeing
Frederick Franck
The Zen of Seeing
By Frederick Franck
A meditation on seeing as a form of contemplation. Franck's approach to drawing not as a technical skill but as a way of truly seeing the world has transformed how I experience everyday life.
The Little Prince
Antoine de Saint-Exup√©ry
The Little Prince
By Antoine de Saint-Exup√©ry
A deceptively simple tale that contains profound truths about human connection. The fox's lesson that "what is essential is invisible to the eye" remains one of the most important principles in my life.
The Artist's Way
Julia Cameron
The Artist's Way
By Julia Cameron
A spiritual path to higher creativity through practical exercises and insights. Cameron's morning pages and artist dates have become essential tools for reconnecting with my creative self.
Leonardo da Vinci
Walter Isaacson
Leonardo da Vinci
By Walter Isaacson
A comprehensive biography that explores da Vinci's extraordinary curiosity and creativity. Isaacson's portrayal of Leonardo's integration of art and science continues to inspire my approach to learning.
Deep Work
Cal Newport
Deep Work
By Cal Newport
A compelling case for focused, distraction-free work in an increasingly fragmented world. Newport's practical strategies for cultivating depth have improved both my productivity and satisfaction.
Range
David Epstein
Range
By David Epstein
A counterintuitive exploration of how generalists often thrive in a specialized world. Epstein's research on the benefits of diverse experiences has validated my own nonlinear path.
Essays & Literary Works
A Room of One's Own
Virginia Woolf
A Room of One's Own
By Virginia Woolf
My introduction to Woolf's brilliant prose. Beyond its feminist themes, this book contains underappreciated gems that form a decent base of my worldview‚Äînot what I expected from the premise.
The Common Reader
Virginia Woolf
The Common Reader
By Virginia Woolf
A collection of essays that demonstrate Woolf's extraordinary insight into literature and life. Her ability to inhabit the minds of authors across centuries continues to astound me.
Emerson's Essays
Ralph Waldo Emerson
Emerson's Essays
By Ralph Waldo Emerson
A treasure trove of wisdom on self-reliance, nature, and the human spirit. There's so much in here that even reading just one essay can be transformative.
Lost in Thought
Zena Hitz
Lost in Thought
By Zena Hitz
A defense of intellectual life for its own sake. Hitz's exploration of how learning can be a refuge and source of dignity has affirmed my belief in the inherent value of contemplation.
The Intellectual Life
A.G. Sertillanges
The Intellectual Life
By A.G. Sertillanges
A guide to cultivating a life of the mind that balances intellectual pursuit with spiritual growth. Sertillanges' practical wisdom on reading, writing, and thinking has shaped my approach to scholarship.
Poetry
Leaves of Grass
Walt Whitman
Leaves of Grass
By Walt Whitman
A revolutionary collection that celebrates the individual and the interconnectedness of all life. Whitman's democratic vision and embrace of the body, soul, and natural world continue to resonate deeply with me.
Howl
Allen Ginsberg
Howl
By Allen Ginsberg
A raw, unfiltered cry against conformity and materialism. Ginsberg's passionate defense of those who live outside conventional boundaries challenged me to question societal norms.
Fiction
The Midnight Library
Matt Haig
The Midnight Library
By Matt Haig
A favorite of my youth that explores the infinite possibilities of life through the story of a library between life and death. It made me consider how seemingly small choices can lead to vastly different lives.
The Raven Cycle Series
Maggie Stiefvater
The Raven Cycle Series
By Maggie Stiefvater
Another favorite from my youth that made the world around me feel a little more magical. These books awakened my appreciation for the mystical possibilities hidden in ordinary places.
Life After Life
Kate Atkinson
Life After Life
By Kate Atkinson
A novel about a woman who lives her life repeatedly, making different choices each time. Its exploration of how small decisions can have enormous consequences has stayed with me.
